text: T-shirts made in Asia, 24-hour Amazon delivery, Russian oil at the local
  pump—these are just some of the examples that fuel the image of a smooth and
  seamless globalization. But this fluidity is merely an illusion, and
  globalization is a series of local frictions constantly negotiated by
  multinational corporations. In the case of fossil fuel companies, these
  frictions could escalate into genuine conflicts during the ecological
  transition.
